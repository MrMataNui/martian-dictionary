    Grammar

    Main word order: Object-Subject-Verb-Oblique. "Mary opened the door with a key" turns into

    The door Mary opened with a key.

    Adjective order: Adjectives are positioned after the noun.

    Adposition: postpositions


    Noun

    Nouns form plural with separate plural word:

    Plural YE /je/
    plural particle

    Nouns have two cases:

    Nominative is the doer of a verb: dog bites man

    Accusative is the done-to of a verb: man bites dog

    Perfect aspect

    Perfect aspect in English is exemplified in ‘I have read this book’,

    which expresses an event that took place before the time spoken

    but which has an effect on or is in some way still relevant to the present.

    In Martian, the perfect is constructed with an affix

    Perfect

    Suffix /-(æ)jɾu/

    SFUJOHOKAYRU /sfuʤɔʔɔcæˈjɾu/
